The science of soil, water & air in working farm fields

About the lab

Led by Dr. Madhav Dhakal, the Environmental Soil Science Lab studies how soil, water, plants, and the atmosphere interact in agricultural systems — combining field experiments, continuous flux monitoring, and process-based modeling to build more productive, resource-efficient, and resilient cropping systems.

Our work spans evapotranspiration, greenhouse-gas emissions, soil health, and climate-smart agriculture, with active rice and corn studies across the Mississippi Delta and Blackbelt.

Research highlights

Greenhouse gas in rice

Static chambers and six flux towers quantify methane and nitrous oxide under tailwater-reuse irrigation.

Nitrogen management in corn

Testing fertility strategies that cut nitrous oxide while protecting yield.

Evapotranspiration & modeling

Eddy covariance plus machine learning to map water and carbon exchange in rice.
