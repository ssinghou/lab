Our team

People

A multidisciplinary group spanning soil science, agronomy, engineering, and environmental monitoring.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Madhav Dhakal

Assistant Research Professor · Lab Director

Dr. Dhakal leads research on soil-plant-water relations, evapotranspiration, greenhouse gas emissions, soil health, and climate-smart agriculture. He earned his Ph.D. in Plant and Soil Science from Texas Tech University and has authored more than 20 peer-reviewed publications.

Email: madhav.dhakal@msstate.edu
Office: (662) 325-2044
Personal website

Postdoctoral Researchers

Dr. Kharla Mendez

Postdoctoral Associate

Dr. Mendez investigates how water-management systems affect crop yield and trace-gas fluxes, and supports sampling, instrumentation, and analysis across the lab's projects.

She earned her Ph.D. in Molecular Biosciences from Arkansas State University (2024); her dissertation developed a high-throughput FTIR method to quantify non-structural carbohydrates in rice under high night-time temperature stress. She is based in Jonesboro, Arkansas.

Email: ksm485@msstate.edu

Dr. Amrit Shrestha

Postdoctoral Research Associate

Dr. Shrestha is an agricultural engineer specializing in UAV remote sensing, geospatial analytics, and data-driven crop modeling — including crop phenology monitoring, management-zone delineation, and machine-learning yield prediction.

He holds a Ph.D. in Agricultural and Biological Engineering from Mississippi State University, an M.S. from KMITL (Thailand), and a B.S. from Kathmandu University (Nepal).

Email: as5085@msstate.edu

Graduate Students

Pankaj Prashad Joshi

Graduate Research Assistant · Ph.D., Agronomy

Pankaj studies greenhouse-gas emissions and climate-smart, regenerative management in rice and corn, using trace-gas analyzers and flux measurements to relate management practices to emissions.

He holds degrees in agriculture (Tribhuvan University) and agronomy (Agriculture and Forestry University, Nepal).

Oluwadamilare Oloyede

Graduate Research Assistant · Ph.D., Agronomy

Oluwadamilare studies soil-plant-atmosphere relationships, focusing on alternate wetting and drying and tailwater recirculation effects on greenhouse-gas emissions and evapotranspiration in rice, pairing eddy covariance with remote sensing.

He holds degrees from the University of Ibadan (Nigeria) and an international joint M.S. in soils and global change (Ghent University, Belgium & Aarhus University, Denmark).
